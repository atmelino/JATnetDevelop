Introduction

The physical basis of a dynamics simulation is Newton's second law: \begin{equation}F=m \cdot a \end{equation} where F is the total force acting on a point object of mass m, and a is the resulting acceleration The acceleration awhich is a function of time: \begin{equation} a=a(t)\end{equation} The acceleration is also the time derivative of the velocity: \begin{equation} a=\frac{dv}{dt}\end{equation} and the velocity is the time derivative of the position: \begin{equation} v=\frac{dx}{dt}\end{equation}
